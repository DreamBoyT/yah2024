Gear Up for Yet Another Hackathon 2k22: SVCE ACM's Third Edition 24-Hour Hackathon!

Yet Another Hackathon 2k22, a 24-hour student-run event hosted by the ACM Student Chapter of Sri Venkateswara College of Engineering, beckons engineering students to tackle real-world challenges and craft innovative solutions. This inclusive hackathon welcomes participants of all skill levels to select problem statements across various domains and collaborate to address pressing issues, fostering a sustainable future.

Moreover, as part of the SVCE ACM W-Wing initiative to empower women in tech, an INR 150 discount is extended to every individual participating in an all-female team, promoting diversity and leveling the playing field in this collaborative technical forum. Join us at YAH'22 for a transformative experience where ideas are nurtured and realities are shaped!

Event Format

The event schedules are listed below to help you familiarize yourself with the event and the venue for each happening.

  • FIRST DAY
  • SECOND DAY
09.00 AM Room 240

Inauguration Ceremony

The commencement of the event marks the inauguration ceremony where esteemed guests will address the students, delivering a warm welcome speech. During this phase, the theme or challenge of the event will be introduced, setting the tone for the ensuing activities. Additionally, teams will be formed, laying the foundation for collaborative efforts.

10.00 AM Room 360

Hacking Begins

Following the inauguration, students will delve into their respective domains, initiating the hacking phase. This period allows participants to commence their work, brainstorming and implementing solutions tailored to the chosen challenge. It marks the beginning of active problem-solving and innovation.

11.00 AM Room 450

First Stage of Evaluation

Throughout the day, participants will engage in rigorous work, developing their projects. At this juncture, the initial evaluation stage occurs, offering students an opportunity for feedback and mentoring. Reviewers provide insights and guidance, aiding students in refining their approaches and strategies.

11.00 AM Room 240

Project Finalization and Submission

On the second day, teams focus on finalizing their projects, ensuring completion before the submission deadline. Once submitted, students conclude their active involvement in the event, transitioning to the evaluation phase. This period underscores the culmination of intensive efforts and the manifestation of innovative solutions.

01.00 PM Room 450

Final Evaluation

During the final evaluation phase, the adjudicating panel, led by the primary judge, meticulously assesses each project. Criteria are meticulously applied, with points allocated based on predefined benchmarks. This rigorous assessment ensures a fair and thorough evaluation, reflecting the quality and ingenuity of each submission.

03.00 PM Room 650

Results Declaration and Closing Ceremony

As the event draws to a close, dignitaries and guest speakers gather for the momentous occasion of results declaration and prize distribution. This phase celebrates the achievements of participants, recognizing exemplary performance and innovative solutions. The closing ceremony signifies the culmination of the event, highlighting the collective efforts and successes of all involved.

Do you have Questions?

Here are the list of questions that are commonly asked.

Who can participate?

YAH 2022 is open to all engineering students, looking to create impactful and cutting edge products.

How much does it cost?

Rs. 650 per person. We'll take care of all your meals & refreshments! You'll pay this only if you're selected for the on-campus round. Online round is free of cost!

Will we get any discounts?

As an initiative taken by the SVCE-ACM W to promote equal participation, we are offering Rs.150 per person discount if your team comprises of only women.

What do we need to bring?

A hackathon is a very hands-on and grassroots event. You will need to bring your laptops, mobile phones and any other devices that you need to design your product.

But above all, come in with a proper night's sleep. Make sure you have your college ID card and your covid vaccination certificate.

Can I use frameworks and APIs?

Of course! As long as your final product is something original, there is no harm in using something to help you achieve that.

Is there reimbursement for travel?

No, we do not offer any reimbursement for travel expenses.

How will the shortlisting be done in the online round?

Our team will evaluate both your abstract to test your problem-solving skills and creativity as well as your GitHub (Or GitLab or BitBucket or anything else). Based on a combination of both of these, we will shortlist teams.

Will I get help if I face any problems?

Of course! We have a lot of mentors coming in from many different fields and all of them are there to help you out.

What do I get out of this hackathon?

You get a great opportunity for peer networking, compete to win 70k worth of cash prizes, chances to gain internships or job offers from Casa Retail AI and Mad Street Den, an amazing learning experience, punny jokes, and some awesome memories. ;)

When and where is the hackathon being conducted?

It is being conducted at Sri Venkateswara College of Engineering, Sriperumbudur on July 30 & 31. Please scroll down to find the exact location.

When is the last date to register?

Registrations will close at 11:59 PM, July 24th, 2022. We will announce the shortlisted teams shortly after that.

How many people can be on each team?

You can have a team of 3-5 members from the same or from different institutions. We recommend having a balanced team with a mixture of designers and developers.

What about food?

We'll be providing you with lunch & dinner on day 1 and breakfast & lunch on day 2. We'll also have a stash of snacks and refreshments available for everyone round the clock.

Is there Wi-Fi?

YES!

What is the judging criteria?

Our judges will be evaluating teams on the 3 I's - Innovation (Originality & Creativity of the idea), Implementation (Technical challenge & completeness of the project), and Impact (Business or Social Impact.)

Do we have to implement the same idea that was mentioned in our abstract?

No, it's not necessary for you to implement the same idea mentioned in your abstract. You are free to implement a different idea on the day of the hackathon.
